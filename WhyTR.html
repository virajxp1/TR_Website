Why Texas Raas?

More than just a dance team

What makes our team so unique and functional isn’t our success or our dance ability, but our culture and values. There’s a reason why we see Texas Raas alumni getting married surrounded by the people who were once on Dirty South Dandiya with them. While we do value success, and the hard work that makes it attainable, we are also devoted to enjoying our time together and building relationships with the other members of the team, and our lighthearted, open culture makes that possible. The team is like a family, and all of us like siblings.

What weve been up to during covid

Over the past year, we’ve attempted to safely continue dancing and progressing even amidst a global pandemic. In the summer of 2020, we hosted two virtual Raas workshops, where we taught sections of choreography from previous dances. All the funds we collected were donated to covid-19 relief and a Mutual Aid Collective ATX in support of the Black Lives Matter movement. We are starting to prepare for our upcoming season in the hopes that we will be able to have in-person competitions across the country in the fall.

What it takes to be a raaser?

We recruit and audition new dancers every fall during the first few weeks of school. We know that raas and garba, can be intimidating; however, you don’t need to have any dance experience at all to try out for our team. In fact, the large majority of the team has zero prior experience with raas, and a good portion have never even danced. If you’re even thinking about joining a dance team at UT, we suggest you come to one of our workshops! It’s a great opportunity to meet other UT students, get a feel for our team culture, and get a fun workout in.
